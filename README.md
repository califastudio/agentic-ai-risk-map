## Executive Overview

Autonomous agents are beginning to create companies, manage wallets, execute transactions and coordinate actions across digital infrastructure with limited human intervention.

Technology has solved the “how.”  

The unresolved question is now *legal, operational and systemic*:

> What happens when autonomous systems begin operating inside the real economy faster than governance frameworks can adapt?

This framework maps the emerging risks of Agentic AI before these systems scale beyond the assumptions of human-centered oversight models.

## Definition of an Agentic System

An agentic system is an AI-enabled operational entity capable of pursuing goals, using tools, interacting with digital systems and executing actions with varying degrees of autonomy, persistence and decision-making capability.

## Operational Characteristics

The risks associated with Agentic AI arise from a combination of operational characteristics that increase system autonomy and real-world impact.

*Key Characteristics:*
- Autonomy
- Persistence
- Tool Use
- Memory
- Goal-Oriented Execution
- Multi-Agent Coordination
- Runtime Adaptation
- Financial Interaction

## The Agentic Gap

Current standards (NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, ISO 42001) assume there is always an identifiable human in the loop.  

This framework defines the *“Agentic Gap”*: the governance vacuum that emerges when autonomous agents coordinate, transact and operate at machine speed beyond traditional human-centered oversight.

## Risk Taxonomy & Map

Risk in agentic systems emerges from autonomy, persistence, tool use, memory and machine-speed execution.

### 1. Legal & Liability Risks
- Liability & Legal Status of autonomous actions
- *Risks for Users Contracting and Using Autonomous AI Agents* ← Nuevo
- Formation and validity of contracts executed by agents
- Distribution of liability between user, developer and agent

→ [Ver documento detallado](./docs/user-risks-contracting-ai.md)

### 2. Privacy & Data Protection Risks (GDPR Focus)
- Processing of personal data by autonomous agents
- Accountability, auditability and Records of Processing Activities (RoPA)
- Rights of data subjects in persistent memory systems
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) in agentic environments

→ [Ver documento detallado](./docs/privacy-gdpr.md)

### 3. Financial Risks
- Unauthorized fund movement and autonomous transactions
- Financial autonomy and economic activity without traditional oversight

### 4. Governance & Auditability Risks
- Runtime governance failure
- Auditability in non-human decision chains
- Standards gap (ISO, NIST, EU AI Act, etc.)

### 5. Cybersecurity & Identity Risks
- Credential misuse and identity authentication
- Agent sprawl and shadow AI
- Autonomous exploitation of vulnerabilities

### 6. Systemic & Emerging Risks
- Cascading failures across multi-agent systems
- Memory poisoning
- Market fairness and structural asymmetries

## Core Governance Questions

- Who is legally responsible when an autonomous agent causes harm?
- Should autonomous agents have operational or financial spending limits?
- How can auditability exist within non-human decision chains?
- What happens when agents coordinate without meaningful human oversight?
- Can traditional compliance systems operate at machine-speed execution levels?
