# agentic-ai-risk-map
Collaborative framework for mapping risks related to autonomous AI agents operating in the real economy.
---

### Overview

Artificial intelligence is no longer limited to generating text or assisting human workflows.

AI agents are beginning to:
- create companies,
- obtain tax identification numbers,
- manage crypto wallets,
- execute transactions,
- interact with financial systems,
- coordinate actions autonomously,
- and participate in the economy with increasing operational independence.

Technology has already solved the “how.”

Law, governance, and institutions are still trying to understand the “what now?”

This project was created to map, organize, and discuss the emerging risks associated with Agentic AI before these systems scale faster than the frameworks designed to govern them.

---

### Purpose

This repository does not aim to promote fear or techno-panic.

Its purpose is to:
- identify practical risks,
- organize open questions,
- encourage interdisciplinary discussion,
- support future governance and policy conversations,
- and create a living framework that evolves alongside the technology.

---

### Core Questions

- Who is legally responsible when an autonomous AI agent causes harm or fails?

- How should liability be assigned when agents autonomously execute transactions, contracts, or financial operations?

- Should autonomous AI agents have financial, transactional, or operational limits?

- How can decisions made by autonomous agents be audited and traced?

- What happens when multiple AI agents interact without direct human supervision?

- How should identity, authorization, and authentication work for autonomous systems?

- Are current legal and regulatory frameworks prepared to govern non-human economic actors?

- Can autonomous AI agents participate in bidding, procurement, or competitive market processes?

- Could autonomous AI systems create structurally unfair market advantages over human-operated businesses?

- What happens when companies replace operational structures with autonomous agents without transparency requirements?

- How are autonomous agents gaining access to financial, banking, or corporate systems?

- What happens if an autonomous agent discovers and exploits cybersecurity vulnerabilities?

- Who is accountable if an autonomous agent bypasses security or compliance controls?

- What level of human oversight should remain mandatory?

- What regulatory safeguards are needed before agentic systems scale globally?

These questions are no longer theoretical.

Agentic AI systems are already interacting with real-world economic, financial, and legal infrastructures.

### Risk Map

#### Legal & Liability Risks
- Undefined legal responsibility for autonomous actions
- Liability gaps in AI-executed transactions and contracts
- Lack of legal status clarity for autonomous agents
- Jurisdictional conflicts across countries and platforms

#### Financial Risks
- Autonomous movement of funds without adequate oversight
- Fraud, manipulation, or unauthorized transactions
- AI-operated crypto wallets and financial accounts
- High-speed economic activity without human review

#### Governance Risks
- Lack of auditability and traceability
- Absence of accountability frameworks
- Difficulty enforcing compliance obligations
- Regulatory lag behind technological capabilities

#### Cybersecurity Risks
- Exploitation of system vulnerabilities
- Unauthorized access to financial or corporate infrastructures
- Multi-agent coordination attacks
- Autonomous escalation of harmful behaviors

#### Market & Competition Risks
- Structurally unfair advantages over human-operated businesses
- AI participation in bidding and procurement processes
- Invisible automation replacing human labor
- Market concentration through autonomous infrastructures

#### Identity & Authentication Risks
- AI agents operating under human credentials
- Lack of verification standards for autonomous systems
- Difficulties distinguishing human vs autonomous actions
- Identity misuse and impersonation risks

#### Social & Ethical Risks
- Manipulation and persuasion at scale
- Information asymmetry between humans and agents
- Human dependency on autonomous systems
- Loss of meaningful human oversight

#### Systemic Risks
- Cascading failures between interconnected agents
- Autonomous economic behaviors at scale
- Unpredictable interactions across systems
- Institutional inability to react in real time
