# Emerging Agentic Risk Categories

As agentic systems evolve from passive chat interfaces into autonomous operational actors, risk analysis must expand beyond hallucinations and model accuracy. Modern agentic environments introduce systemic, runtime and multi-agent risks that require continuous governance, observability and control.

---

## 1. Agent Sprawl

The uncontrolled proliferation of agents, tools, workflows, MCP servers and automations across organizations.

### Risks
- Unknown active agents
- Shadow AI systems
- Untracked permissions
- Decentralized workflows
- Loss of governance visibility

---

## 2. Blast Radius & Cascading Failures

In agentic systems, a single incorrect decision can propagate across connected systems, APIs, tools and other agents.

### Risks
- Multi-system operational failures
- Autonomous propagation of errors
- Financial or infrastructure damage
- Chain-of-action escalation

---

## 3. Runtime Governance

Traditional AI governance focused on pre-deployment evaluation. Agentic systems require continuous runtime monitoring and intervention.

### Key Concepts
- Real-time observability
- Continuous monitoring
- Approval gates
- Human-in-the-loop escalation
- Runtime isolation
- Kill switches

---

## 4. Least Agency & Least Privilege

Agents should only possess the minimum autonomy and permissions necessary to perform a task.

### Risks
- Privilege escalation
- Unauthorized actions
- Excessive tool access
- Autonomous misuse of systems

---

## 5. Memory Poisoning & Context Manipulation

Persistent memory systems can be manipulated through malicious or false contextual inputs.

### Risks
- Long-term behavioral corruption
- Prompt injection persistence
- False memory retention
- Decision manipulation

---

## 6. Agent Supply Chain Risk

Agentic ecosystems increasingly depend on external MCP servers, plugins, APIs and third-party tools.

### Risks
- Compromised external services
- Untrusted integrations
- Dependency vulnerabilities
- Tool-chain attacks

---

## 7. Responsibility Gaps

Agentic decision-making distributes operational influence across models, developers, users, organizations and external tools.

### Key Governance Question
Who is accountable when autonomous systems cause harm?

---

## 8. Auditability & Traceability

Agentic systems require reconstructable decision histories.

### Requirements
- Action logging
- Tool call tracing
- Decision reconstruction
- State-change visibility
- Explainability layers

---

## 9. Multi-Agent Coordination Risk

Networks of interacting agents may generate emergent and unpredictable behaviors.

### Risks
- Recursive autonomy
- Conflicting goals
- Emergent unsafe behavior
- Coordination failures

---

## Closing Reflection

> Agentic governance is no longer limited to model behavior. It now requires controlling autonomy, permissions, memory, tool usage, runtime operations and systemic interactions across interconnected agents.
