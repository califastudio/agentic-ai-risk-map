# Risks for Users Contracting and Using Autonomous AI Agents

## Introduction
When a user or company contracts, deploys, or delegates tasks to an autonomous AI agent, they assume a series of legal, financial, and operational risks that are currently *poorly regulated and ill-defined*. This document maps the main risks from the user's perspective.

*Core question*: What risks am I accepting (consciously or unconsciously) when using an AI agent?

---

## Key Risks

### 1. Liability for the Agent’s Actions
- Civil and criminal liability for actions performed by the agent (contracts, transactions, publications, decisions).
- Is the user liable even if they are not supervising in real time?
- Possible classification as "act of the thing" or "created risk".

### 2. Formation and Validity of Contracts
- Are contracts entered into by an AI agent without direct human intervention legally valid?
- Risk of nullity or challenge due to lack of real consent or defects in consent.
- Issues with electronic signature and attribution of intent.

### 3. Contractual Risks with the Agent Provider
- Abusive clauses in the provider’s Terms of Service (limitation of liability, indemnity, etc.).
- Risk transfer to the end user.
- Difficulty negotiating terms when using third-party agents.

### 4. Lack of Transparency and Information
- Users who do not fully understand the agent’s capabilities, limitations, and risks.
- Insufficient "informed consent" before enabling autonomy.

### 5. Financial and Credit Risks
- Agent generating debts, losses, or financial commitments in the user’s name.
- Impact on credit score or creditworthiness.

### 6. Insurance Coverage
- Do existing insurance policies (civil liability, cyber, professional) cover damages caused by AI agents?
- Possible exclusions for "autonomy" or "artificial intelligence".

### 7. Regulatory and Sanction Risks
- Regulatory breaches committed by the agent (AML, consumer protection, competition law, etc.).
- Sanctions falling on the user or company.

---

## Initial Mitigation Recommendations
- Set clear autonomy limits and human approval thresholds.
- Maintain complete and auditable logs of agent decisions.
- Carefully review the provider’s Terms of Service.
- Include specific liability clauses in contracts with clients.
- Evaluate the need for specialized AI insurance.
